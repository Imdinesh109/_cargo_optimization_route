Feature Contribution Reference — Cargo Routing Optimization (CO-008)

Target Variable:
optimization_score

Primary Logic:
Higher optimization scores represent operationally superior routes.

Core Feature Contributions:

1. cost_sar
- Lower cost improves optimization score.
- High cost routes receive penalties unless justified by Express routing.

2. total_transit_time_hours
- Faster routes receive higher scores.
- Longer transit duration reduces route attractiveness.

3. reliability_score
- Higher reliability strongly increases optimization score.
- Reliability is reduced by:
  - More connections
  - Weather risk
  - Economy routing

4. weather_risk_score
- Higher weather risk reduces optimization score.
- Weather risk indirectly lowers reliability.

5. num_connections
- Direct routes preferred.
- More transshipments reduce reliability and increase transit risk.

6. capacity_available_kg
- Routes must have sufficient available capacity.
- Near-capacity routes receive penalties.

7. load_factor
- High load factor reduces operational flexibility.
- Lower load factor improves optimization score.

8. priority
- Express routes prioritize reliability and speed.
- Economy routes prioritize lower cost.

9. cargo_type and shc_code
- Special handling cargo receives additional operational constraints.
- Dangerous goods and heavy cargo often receive slight penalties.
- Pharmaceutical and perishable cargo receive stricter optimization evaluation.

Approximate Relative Importance:
- Cost Efficiency: 24%
- Transit Time: 24%
- Reliability: 22%
- Weather Risk: 10%
- Connections: 8%
- Capacity: 7%
- Load Factor: 5%

Additional Noise:
Small Gaussian noise added to preserve realism and avoid perfect deterministic behavior.
